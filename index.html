Nissan GT-R35

Nissan GT-R, is a high-performance sports car produced by the Japanese automaker Nissan. It was introduced in December 2007 as the successor to the Nissan Skyline GT-R, and it quickly gained a reputation for its impressive performance, handling, and cutting-edge technology.

Design

The GT-R's design is characterized by its aggressive and aerodynamic appearance. The car features a long, low-slung body with a distinctive front grille, large air intakes, and a rear wing for enhanced downforce.

Engine

The GT-R is powered by a twin-turbocharged 3.8-liter V6 engine, which has been hand-assembled by master technicians. In its early iterations, it produced around 480 horsepower and 430 lb-ft of torque.

Transmission

The GT-R is equipped with a sophisticated dual-clutch six-speed automatic transmission. It also features an advanced all-wheel-drive system that provides excellent traction and stability, contributing to its impressive handling capabilities.
